1 - Retail Ecosystem

Retailing is the set of activities that markets products and services to final consumers for their own personal or household use.


It does this by organizing their availability on a relatively large scale and supplying them to consumers on a relatively small scale.


SOURCE: Retailing: environmen & operations (pages 12 – 15)

To make retailing possible, an ecosystem of various participants is needed.


1.1 - Relationships in the Retail Ecosystem

The figure here shows a typical retail ecosystem. A retail ecosystem describes the relationships between the various actors as shown below.

✖

Supplier

The Supplier provides supplies to the Retailer directly or via the Logistics Provider.

✖

Logistics Provider

The Logistics Provider will take care of transporting the supply of goods from the Supplier to the Retailer, and from the Retailer to the Consumer.

✖

Retailer

The Retailer procures from the Supplier, and sells it to the Consumer.

✖

Consumer

The Consumer buys products and services from the Retailer.

✖

Regulator

The Regulator ensures that all products and services are provided according to existing rules and regulations.

✖

Finance Provider

The Finance Provider can finance both the Retailer and Consumer. For e.g., the Consumer can buy on credit, say, interest-free with a credit card.
